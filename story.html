Our History

Learn the history of CATH over the past 30 years

Nearly three decades ago, in the harsh winter of 1991, a group of devoted church parishioners, led by four remarkable and determined women, were moved by the plight of those sleeping rough in Perth. Fueled by their deep concern and boundless compassion, they took action, giving birth to Churches Action for the Homeless (CATH). This compassionate endeavor began with a humble soup kitchen, staffed entirely by dedicated volunteers, including the original quartet of ladies who have since earned the endearing title of the 'Founding Mothers of CATH'.

In the early days, CATH operated on a modest scale, offering basic services such as meals, clothing, and a safe space for those experiencing homelessness. Their first shelter was a small church hall where volunteers worked tirelessly to provide warmth and comfort to those in need.

As the years passed, CATH's mission evolved. Recognizing that homelessness was a complex issue with deep-seated roots, they expanded their services to include counseling, addiction support, and job training programs. This holistic approach aimed not only to provide immediate relief but also to empower individuals to regain stability and independence.

By the 2010s, CATH had become a cornerstone of the Perth & Kinross community. Their outreach extended beyond shelters and into the streets, where dedicated teams engaged with those living rough, offering them not only immediate assistance but also the prospect of a brighter future.

As the organization continued to grow, CATH leveraged its influence to advocate for systemic change. They worked closely with local government agencies, leveraging their expertise and firsthand experience to shape policies that addressed the underlying causes of homelessness.

Today, CATH stands as a testament to the power of collective action and unwavering compassion. Through decades of dedication and resilience, they have not only provided essential support to those experiencing homelessness but also inspired a community to come together in the fight against this pressing issue. Their story serves as a beacon of hope, reminding us all that positive change is possible when individuals and communities work hand in hand.
